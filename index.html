Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our auto body repair services

How long does a typical repair take?

Repair times vary depending on the extent of damage. Minor repairs might take 1-3 days, while major collision repairs could take 1-2 weeks. We provide accurate time estimates after a thorough inspection of your vehicle. Our team works efficiently while never compromising on quality.

Do you work with insurance companies?

Yes, we work with all major insurance providers. We can help you navigate the claims process and communicate directly with your insurance company to ensure a smooth repair experience. Our team is experienced in dealing with insurance adjusters and will advocate for proper repairs on your behalf.

What warranty do you offer on repairs?

We offer a lifetime warranty on all our repairs for as long as you own your vehicle. Paint jobs come with a 3-year warranty against fading or peeling. All warranties cover both parts and labor. This warranty is transferable to subsequent owners if you sell your vehicle.

Do you use original manufacturer parts?

We primarily use OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) parts to ensure proper fit and finish. However, we can discuss alternative options if requested. All parts we use meet or exceed manufacturer specifications for quality and safety. We'll always review parts options with you before proceeding with repairs.

Can you match my vehicle's paint color exactly?

Yes, our computerized color matching system ensures a perfect match to your vehicle's original paint. We account for factors like age, sun fading, and environmental wear to blend new paint seamlessly with the existing finish. Our paint specialist will perform test sprays to verify the match before final application.

How do I know if my frame is damaged?

Our laser measuring system can detect even minor frame misalignment that may not be visible to the naked eye. Signs of potential frame damage include uneven tire wear, the vehicle pulling to one side, or unusual noises. We recommend a free frame inspection after any significant impact.
